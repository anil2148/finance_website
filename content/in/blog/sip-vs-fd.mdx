## Quick answer
SIP is usually better for long-term wealth building (5+ years) when you can tolerate volatility. FD is better for capital stability and near-term goals where downside risk is unacceptable.

## India context that matters
- FD returns are usually fixed and taxed at slab rate.
- Equity mutual fund SIP gains are market-linked and taxed under equity capital-gains rules.
- For goals like a ₹10 lakh corpus over 8–10 years, SIP often has higher expected upside but no guarantees.

## Example in lakh terms
If you invest ₹20,000/month for 10 years:
- At 6.8% annualized (FD-like): about ₹33 lakh
- At 11% annualized (equity SIP assumption): about ₹41 lakh

Use this as planning math, not guaranteed outcome.

## Decision framework
1. If goal is below 3 years: prefer FD or debt mix.
2. If goal is 5+ years and inflation matters: SIP is usually stronger.
3. Keep emergency fund and short-term obligations outside equity SIP.
