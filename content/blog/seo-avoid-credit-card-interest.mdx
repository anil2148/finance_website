## Interest avoidance is a system, not willpower
You avoid interest by protecting the grace period every single cycle. One missed due date or partial statement payment can reset progress.

## Three settings to lock in immediately
1. **Autopay statement balance** (not minimum due).
2. **Mid-cycle utilization payment** if your balance spikes above 30% of limit.
3. **Spending alert** when you cross your monthly card budget.

## Example with statement timing
If your statement closes on the 20th and due date is the 15th:
- Pay down large purchases before the 20th to reduce reported utilization.
- Keep autopay for full statement amount on the 15th.
This preserves on-time history and keeps utilization trends cleaner.

## Common failure points
- Carrying a “small balance” to build credit (myth).
- Turning off autopay after a checking-account change.
- Using intro APR cards without a payoff date.
