Where you store savings should match your timeline and liquidity needs. Yield matters, but access speed and account rules matter too.

## Quick framework

- Emergency fund: HYSA for fast access and FDIC insurance.
- 3–12 month goals: HYSA or money market funds.
- Known date goals: CDs if you can lock funds.
- Daily spending cash: checking only for short-term transaction needs.

## What to compare before opening

- APY and how often it changes.
- Transfer limits and withdrawal friction.
- Fees and minimum-balance rules.
- Mobile experience and transfer speed.

A slightly lower yield with better access can be smarter for emergency funds.

## Match account type to job

Emergency cash needs speed and stability, so HYSA usually wins. Goal-based money with known dates can accept lower liquidity in exchange for yield certainty through CDs. Keep this decision job-based and revisit only when your goals or timeline changes.

Do not let analysis delay action: pick one account setup today, automate transfers, and refine later only if account rules or yields materially change.

This keeps your cash strategy simple: liquidity first for emergencies, yield second, and lockups only when timeline certainty is high.
