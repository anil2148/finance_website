Most beginners do not lose because of one bad stock pick. They lose because their process is fragile. This roadmap is built as an execution calendar so your plan survives both calm and volatile markets.

## Quarter 1: Build the base (weeks 1–12)

- Set emergency cash baseline before increasing risk exposure.
- Open core accounts and automate contributions.
- Choose a simple portfolio design you can explain in one paragraph.

If cash reserves are thin, begin with smaller contributions and increase gradually.

## Quarter 2: Raise contribution quality (weeks 13–24)

- Capture any employer retirement match.
- Review platform and fund fees.
- Add one automatic contribution increase trigger (for each raise or bonus).

This quarter is about consistency, not complexity.

## Quarter 3: Improve risk behavior (weeks 25–36)

Create a written response for market declines:
- at -10%, continue normal contributions
- at -20%, rebalance on schedule
- at -30%, avoid panic changes unless your life circumstances changed

Predefined rules reduce emotional decisions.

## Quarter 4: Tax and allocation review (weeks 37–52)

- Check whether account placement is tax-aware.
- Rebalance if drift is meaningful.
- Decide next-year contribution target based on current cash flow.

## Example progression

Start at $300/month and increase by $50 each quarter:
- Q1: $300
- Q2: $350
- Q3: $400
- Q4: $450

The year ends at $450/month with far lower dropout risk than an aggressive day-one target.

## Decision checkpoints

Before you increase risk, confirm:
1. I can keep contributions running during a bad month.
2. My emergency reserve is not dependent on market gains.
3. I know exactly when I will rebalance and why.

If any answer is no, strengthen process first.
