Most first-year investors do not fail because of one bad fund. They fail because their plan collapses when life gets noisy. Build a system that survives that noise.

## Your first-year map by quarter

### Quarter 1: infrastructure, not optimization
- Open the accounts you actually need.
- Set a contribution amount you can sustain for 6 months.
- Keep emergency reserves intact before raising risk.

### Quarter 2: automate and simplify
- Turn on recurring contributions.
- Use a small, understandable core allocation.
- Track total costs (fund fees + platform fee + advisory layer).

### Quarter 3: write your volatility playbook
- Define what you will do at -10%, -20%, and -30% market declines.
- Decide rebalancing thresholds in advance.
- Ban ad-hoc allocation changes from headlines alone.

### Quarter 4: annual review and reset
- Review fee drag and account placement.
- Compare target vs actual contribution totals.
- Set a next-year contribution ladder.

## Example contribution ladder (realistic, not heroic)

Start at **$250/month** and increase only when cash flow supports it.

- Q1: $250/month
- Q2: $300/month
- Q3: $350/month
- Q4: $400/month

A stable ladder usually beats an aggressive start that gets abandoned.

## Three decisions that prevent beginner mistakes

1. **If markets fall:** continue contributions unless your job or emergency status changed.
2. **If you get a raise:** split the increase (part lifestyle, part investing) instead of all-or-nothing.
3. **If fees are unclear:** pause new money to that account until full fee transparency is confirmed.
