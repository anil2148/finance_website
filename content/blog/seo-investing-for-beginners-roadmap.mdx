## Start with investor type and timeline
Strong returns usually come from behavior consistency, low costs, and staying invested—not prediction. Choose your lane:
- **Starter investor:** automate contributions first, then optimize fund mix.
- **Optimizer:** lower fees, improve tax location, and rebalance with rules.
- **Near-withdrawal investor:** reduce sequence risk and increase cash-flow resilience.

## Risk, fee, and tax tradeoffs to model
Before changing funds or platforms, answer:
1. What drawdown can you tolerate without panic selling?
2. What are your all-in annual costs (expense ratios + platform + advisory fees)?
3. Are taxable and tax-advantaged accounts being used in the right order?

## Scenario example
If you invest $600/month for 20 years at 7%:
- portfolio A with 0.08% costs can end meaningfully higher than
- portfolio B with 0.85% all-in costs.
Fee gaps look small monthly but large over decades.
