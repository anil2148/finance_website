New investors usually fail from inconsistent behavior, not from one bad ticker. This framework is built around decisions you repeat all year.

## 12-month timeline at a glance

- **Months 1–2:** set emergency baseline and open accounts.
- **Months 3–4:** automate contributions and choose simple core funds.
- **Months 5–6:** raise savings rate after cash-flow review.
- **Months 7–9:** define downturn behavior rules in writing.
- **Months 10–12:** rebalance and set next-year targets.

## Contribution ladder example

Start at $250/month. Increase by $50 every quarter if cash flow allows.

- Q1: $250
- Q2: $300
- Q3: $350
- Q4: $400

This progression is easier to sustain than an aggressive day-one target.

## Decision rules for volatility

Write these before markets drop:

- At -10%: continue normal contributions.
- At -20%: rebalance to target allocation.
- At -30%: make no allocation change unless your life circumstances changed.

Pre-committed rules reduce panic mistakes.

## Minimum annual review checklist

- Fee check on accounts and funds.
- Asset allocation drift check.
- Emergency fund check before increasing risk.
- Tax placement review for next year contributions.
