## Month 1–2: Build the base before buying anything
Start with emergency reserves and account order. Capture any employer 401(k) match, then evaluate Roth IRA vs taxable brokerage based on tax bracket and flexibility needs.

## Month 3–4: Choose a portfolio you can hold through drawdowns
Most beginners do well with a simple 2–3 fund mix. A practical starting point is 80/20 stocks/bonds for long horizons, adjusting lower if volatility triggers panic selling.

## Month 5–8: Improve process quality
- Automate contributions on payday.
- Review allocation monthly, rebalance quarterly.
- Track total fee stack (fund + platform + advisor).

## Month 9–12: Layer tax and behavior controls
Increase contribution rate after raises, avoid strategy-hopping, and document your “when markets fall” rules ahead of time.

## Example progression
Starting at $400/month and raising by $50 each quarter builds discipline and can materially change long-term outcomes without one large lifestyle cut.
