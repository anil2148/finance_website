A strong 401(k) strategy is not "max it out or fail." The right strategy is the highest contribution rate you can hold through normal life friction without pausing every few months.

## Step 1: lock the employer match floor

If your plan matches up to a percentage of salary, that threshold is your minimum target. Contributing below the full match is equivalent to declining part of your compensation package.

Use this order:

1. Contribute enough to collect the full match.
2. Keep emergency reserves and debt minimums stable.
3. Increase your rate in scheduled increments.

## Step 2: convert percentages into paychecks

Percentages feel abstract; paychecks are real. Translate each 1% increase into estimated monthly take-home impact, then choose a level that does not force cash flow stress.

For many households, a 1% pre-tax increase is manageable when paired with automatic payroll deductions. The key is consistency, not aggressive starts followed by reversals.

## Step 3: set annual escalation now

A durable method is:

- Start at your match floor (or current rate if already above it)
- Add **+1% each year** on a fixed date
- Recheck only during major life changes (job change, new childcare costs, etc.)

This prevents retirement contributions from being crowded out by lifestyle creep as income rises.

## Traditional vs Roth 401(k): decision shortcut

Use a simple tie-breaker:

- Expect materially higher taxable income in retirement? Roth may be attractive.
- Expect lower taxable income later? Traditional may provide stronger current-year tax relief.

Many savers also split contributions across both for tax diversification.

## Costly errors to avoid

- Stopping permanently at the match and never escalating
- Taking early withdrawals that trigger taxes/penalties and reset compounding
- Leaving allocations on plan defaults that do not match your time horizon
