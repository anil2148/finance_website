## Why the 50/30/20 rule works (and where it breaks)
The rule is useful because it gives you a fast default: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings/debt goals. But it often fails in high-cost cities or when debt minimums are unusually high.

## Adapted version with real numbers
If your take-home pay is $5,200:
- Needs: $2,900 (56%)
- Wants: $1,000 (19%)
- Goals: $1,300 (25%)

This can still be a healthy plan if your “needs” are temporarily elevated and you are intentionally using the extra goal percentage to clear debt.

## Practical adjustments to keep momentum
- **High rent market:** Cap wants first, then renegotiate fixed bills every 90 days.
- **Debt-heavy phase:** Move to a 60/15/25 split until high-interest balances are gone.
- **Irregular income:** Set percentages, not fixed dollar amounts.
