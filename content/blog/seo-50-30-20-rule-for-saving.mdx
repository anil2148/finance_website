The 50/30/20 rule fails most often when housing jumps or income drops. Instead of forcing the same ratio every month, use **trigger rules** that tell you exactly when to change your split.

## The 4 triggers that decide your next ratio

Use last month’s numbers and run these in order.

1. **Housing trigger:** if housing + utilities exceed 38% of take-home pay, move to a defensive split.
2. **Income trigger:** if total pay is down more than 10% from the three-month average, switch to paycheck allocation.
3. **Debt trigger:** if any debt APR is above 20%, add a temporary payoff sprint.
4. **Stability trigger:** if all three are clear for 90 days, return toward growth mode.

## Split menu (pick one, do not mix)

- **Defensive month:** 65% needs / 10% wants / 25% goals
- **Debt sprint month:** 62% needs / 8% wants / 30% goals
- **Recovery month after income dip:** 60% needs / 15% wants / 25% goals
- **Stable month:** 55% needs / 20% wants / 25% goals

## Case file: Chicago renter with variable overtime

- Net pay average: $4,850/month
- New rent + utilities: $2,010
- Credit card APR: 24.9%

Decision:
- Trigger 1 and 3 are both true.
- Household selects **debt sprint month** for 12 weeks.
- Wants category is capped at $390/month and all overtime goes to the highest APR card.

Outcome after one quarter:
- Card minimum falls by $115/month.
- Budget returns to 60/15/25 without cancelling automatic savings.

## What to automate first

- Fixed payday transfer to savings (even if small).
- Calendar reminder to review split on the 25th of each month.
- One rule for irregular income: at least 50% of bonus/refund cash goes to goals.
