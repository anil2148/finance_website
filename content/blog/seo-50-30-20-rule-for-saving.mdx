## Treat 50/30/20 as a framework, not a strict law
In high-cost markets, needs can exceed 50%. That does not mean the method failed. It means you should adapt the percentages intentionally.

## Three common adaptations
- **Debt-heavy phase:** 55/20/25 (needs/wants/savings+debt payoff).
- **High-rent city:** 60/20/20 while you work on housing-cost strategy.
- **Aggressive saver:** 45/25/30 once fixed costs are optimized.

## Monthly example
Take-home pay: $4,500
- Needs: $2,475
- Wants: $900
- Savings/debt goals: $1,125

Split the goal bucket into emergency fund, retirement, and debt acceleration.
