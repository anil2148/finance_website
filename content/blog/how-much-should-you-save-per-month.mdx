There is no one perfect monthly savings number. A useful target depends on income, fixed costs, age, and what goals you need to fund first.

Think in ranges and systems, not one static percentage.

## Savings rate by income and life stage

- Early career: prioritize consistency and habit formation.
- Mid-career: increase rate as income rises and debt falls.
- Late career: emphasize retirement catch-up plus liquidity planning.

## Goal-based framework

Set one target for each bucket:

- emergency cash,
- medium-term spending goals,
- long-term investing.

Then automate the monthly total transfer so saving happens before discretionary spending expands.

## Start with a practical baseline

If unsure, pick a sustainable percentage now, then raise by 1%–2% after each salary increase.

## How to personalize your number

If your fixed costs are high today, start with a lower but consistent savings amount and increase on schedule. If your fixed costs are moderate, set a higher baseline now and direct raises into long-term investing. The best target is one you can automate and sustain.
