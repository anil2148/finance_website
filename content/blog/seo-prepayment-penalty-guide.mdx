## Who this is for
Borrowers who plan to pay a loan off early, refinance, or make extra principal payments.

## Why this matters
A prepayment penalty can reduce or erase the benefit of refinancing or early payoff. You need to evaluate this fee before choosing a lender.

## How prepayment penalties are structured
Common structures include:
- Percentage of remaining balance
- Fixed months of interest
- Sliding schedule (higher in year 1, lower later)

## How to evaluate the tradeoff
1. Estimate interest saved from paying off early.
2. Subtract any prepayment penalty.
3. Confirm if net savings remain positive.

If savings are small after fees, your cash may be better deployed elsewhere.

## Example scenario
A borrower refinances a $20,000 balance and expects $1,100 in interest savings. If the contract imposes a $700 prepayment penalty, net benefit is only $400 before other closing costs.

## Common mistakes
- Assuming all loans allow free early payoff
- Not checking whether extra monthly payments trigger penalty rules
- Focusing on interest rate and ignoring fee provisions

## Next steps
- Request the exact prepayment clause in writing.
- Compare at least three offers including no-penalty options.
- Calculate total net savings before refinancing.
