If you’ve ever guessed at the Roth vs traditional toggle, this guide is for you. The decision is usually less about vibes and more about tax-rate timing. In plain English: compare your **tax bracket now** with the bracket you expect in retirement.

## When Roth wins

Roth 401(k) contributions can win when:

- You are currently in a lower bracket.
- You expect materially higher income later.
- You have a long investing horizon.
- Your cash flow can handle paying taxes now.

Mini-example: if you are in roughly the **12% to 22% range today** but expect withdrawals taxed at **24%+ later**, paying tax now via Roth can be the better long-term move.

## When traditional wins

Traditional 401(k) contributions usually win when:

- You are currently in a high bracket.
- You expect lower taxable income in retirement.
- You want to reduce this year’s tax bill.

Mini-example: if your marginal rate is around **32% now** and you expect retirement withdrawals closer to **22%**, traditional often creates better tax math.

## Break-even logic

If your tax rate now is about the same as your tax rate later, pure tax math is often close to a tie.

In that tie zone:

- **Roth** gives more flexibility for tax-free qualified withdrawals later.
- **Traditional** gives immediate tax savings today.

So the “best” answer can come down to cash-flow preference and planning flexibility, not just one formula.

## Scenarios

### Early-career, mid-income professional

Situation: age 29, stable income, currently near the 22% bracket, expects higher earnings over time.

Recommendation: **lean Roth** for at least part of contributions. Why: today’s bracket is likely lower than future retirement-period withdrawal brackets.

### Late-career, high-income professional

Situation: age 56, peak earnings, currently around a 32%–35% bracket, expects lower taxable income after full-time work.

Recommendation: **lean traditional**. Why: current deductions are very valuable at high marginal rates, and future withdrawal years may be taxed lower.

## What should you do now?

- If you are in a lower bracket now → **lean Roth**.
- If you are in a higher bracket now → **lean traditional**.
- If you expect uncertain future taxes → **split contributions**.
- If you are near a bracket edge → model one change before adjusting payroll elections.
- If unsure → run a **50/50 split for one year** and review.
