A monthly expense audit is broader than a subscription cleanup. It reviews fixed bills, variable essentials, and lifestyle categories so your plan reflects real behavior.

Think of this as your monthly control room: you are not judging every purchase, you are deciding where your next dollar should go.

## What this system includes

Each month, review four groups:

- fixed expenses (housing, insurance, debt minimums),
- variable essentials (groceries, gas, utilities),
- lifestyle spending (dining, shopping, entertainment),
- recurring charges and subscriptions.

## 30-minute workflow you can keep doing

1. Export last month’s transactions.
2. Compare planned vs actual by category.
3. Flag top 3 overruns.
4. Pick one cut and one redirect for next month.
5. Automate the redirect transfer on payday.

## What to track each month

- Total spending and total savings.
- Savings rate trend.
- Repeating leakage categories.
- One structural fix (downgrade, cancellation, rule, or cap).

A structural fix beats one-time willpower every time.

## Monthly scoreboard

Keep one simple scoreboard: planned spending, actual spending, and the delta. After three months, patterns become obvious and you can fix root causes instead of patching symptoms.

A good audit culture is not about perfect months; it is about making next month measurably better than last month.
