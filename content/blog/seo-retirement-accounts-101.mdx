## Account order affects long-term net wealth
Contribution sequence matters because match rules, tax treatment, and withdrawal flexibility differ across account types.

## Common priority sequence
1. Employer plan up to full match.
2. High-interest debt management if APR is extreme.
3. IRA (Roth or Traditional based on bracket and eligibility).
4. Additional 401(k)/403(b) contributions.
5. Taxable brokerage for flexible goals.

## Example decision
A household in a higher current bracket may benefit from Traditional contributions now, while a lower bracket saver may prefer Roth for future tax-free withdrawals.

## Mistakes to avoid
- Skipping employer match.
- Maxing taxable accounts before tax-advantaged space.
- Ignoring fees inside workplace plans.
