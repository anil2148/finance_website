## A simple contribution order most households can use
1. Contribute enough to get full employer 401(k) match.
2. Fund high-interest debt payoff if APR is punitive.
3. Max IRA (Roth or Traditional, based on tax bracket and eligibility).
4. Return to 401(k) for additional tax-advantaged savings.
5. Add taxable brokerage for flexibility.

## Choosing Roth vs Traditional in plain terms
- Roth is often stronger when current tax rate is lower than expected retirement tax rate.
- Traditional can help high earners reduce current taxable income.
- Many households benefit from having both "tax buckets" long term.

## Fee and investment menu checks
Inside workplace plans, watch for:
- High-cost default funds
- Recordkeeping/admin fees
- Limited diversification options

Even a good account type can be undermined by poor fund selection.
