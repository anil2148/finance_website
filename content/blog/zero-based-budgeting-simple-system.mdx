Zero-based budgeting means every dollar is assigned on purpose before the month begins. Income minus planned categories equals zero—not because you spend everything, but because nothing is left directionless.

## Simple monthly system

1. Start with expected take-home income.
2. Assign essentials first.
3. Assign goals (savings, debt payoff, sinking funds).
4. Assign lifestyle spending.
5. Leave no unassigned dollars.

## Short example

If take-home pay is $4,500:

- Essentials: $2,500
- Goals: $1,200
- Lifestyle: $800

Total assigned = $4,500.

## Easy template to keep using

Keep the same four buckets every month: Essentials, Goals, Lifestyle, and Irregular Bills. Most people fail by reinventing categories monthly. Keep structure stable and adjust only amounts.

## Why zero-based works for beginners

Beginners often struggle because "extra" money gets spent by default. Zero-based budgeting solves that by assigning surplus dollars to savings or debt the moment income arrives. That single behavioral shift can increase annual savings without any income change.

Review once per week for five minutes so small category drift does not turn into month-end surprises.

If income varies, run the same framework with conservative income assumptions and allocate upside only after essentials and goals are fully funded.
