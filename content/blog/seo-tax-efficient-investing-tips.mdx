## Tax efficiency is a portfolio design choice
After-tax return depends on asset location, turnover, holding period, and withdrawal sequencing—not just fund selection.

## Core moves with the biggest impact
1. Place tax-inefficient assets in tax-advantaged accounts where possible.
2. Minimize unnecessary short-term gains in taxable accounts.
3. Use tax-loss harvesting rules consistently, not reactively.

## Scenario example
An investor holding bond funds in taxable while equity index funds sit in retirement accounts may improve after-tax outcomes by rethinking location, even if total allocation stays the same.

## Common mistakes
- Harvesting losses then repurchasing too quickly with substantially identical exposure.
- Ignoring turnover and distributions in taxable funds.
- Letting tax complexity delay basic contribution discipline.
