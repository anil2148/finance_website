Tax efficiency is a process, not a year-end scramble. The goal is to keep more after-tax return without adding fragile complexity.

## Your policy in 3 rules

Create a short policy note with:

1. **Asset-location rule:** what belongs in taxable vs tax-advantaged accounts.
2. **Turnover rule:** what changes justify realizing gains.
3. **Rebalance rule:** where rebalancing happens first and how often.

## Practical placement logic

- Keep lower-turnover core holdings in taxable accounts when suitable.
- Use tax-advantaged accounts for strategies with higher tax drag.
- Rebalance in sheltered accounts first to reduce taxable events.

## Mini scenario

You can invest $10,000 this year and must choose:

- add to taxable brokerage, or
- fill remaining tax-advantaged space first.

Decision depends on expected holding period, current tax situation, and withdrawal flexibility. Model both and choose the better after-tax path.

## Guardrails for loss harvesting

Harvest only when all are true:

- Replacement investment is predefined.
- Wash-sale controls are understood.
- Trade supports long-term allocation, not short-term noise.

Without these guardrails, “tax strategy” becomes overtrading.

## Annual maintenance checklist

- Review realized gains/losses before year-end.
- Check for unexpected distributions.
- Reconfirm account locations still match your plan.
- Update rebalancing thresholds as portfolio grows.
