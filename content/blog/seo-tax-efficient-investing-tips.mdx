Many investors focus only on pre-tax returns. Over long periods, tax drag can quietly remove a meaningful portion of performance—especially in taxable accounts with unnecessary turnover.

## Where tax efficiency actually comes from
Three levers usually matter most:

1. **Asset location:** place tax-inefficient holdings in tax-advantaged accounts when possible.
2. **Holding period discipline:** short-term gains are often more costly than long-term gains.
3. **Turnover control:** frequent changes can increase taxable events without improving outcomes.

## A practical location framework
- Taxable account: broad, low-turnover index exposure
- Tax-advantaged account: higher-yield or higher-turnover assets when appropriate
- Rebalancing: prefer tax-sheltered accounts first to reduce taxable realization

This is a framework, not a universal rule. Your account mix and future tax expectations matter.

## Tax-loss harvesting: useful, but rule-based
Harvesting can help if you run it systematically. Problems happen when investors harvest randomly or accidentally repurchase substantially identical exposure too quickly.

Set clear rules:
- predefine acceptable replacement funds
- document holding windows
- avoid ad-hoc trades triggered by headlines

## Common mistakes
- Obsessing over tax optimization before contribution consistency is solved
- Ignoring fund distributions in taxable accounts
- Letting tax fear prevent needed rebalancing
