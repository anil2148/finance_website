Tax efficiency is mostly about avoiding unnecessary friction. You do not need complex tactics—you need a clear order of operations you can repeat without emotion.

## Create a one-page tax policy for your portfolio

Include three rules:

1. **Asset location:** what goes in taxable vs tax-advantaged accounts
2. **Rebalance order:** where you rebalance first to reduce taxable events
3. **Gain realization threshold:** what level of benefit justifies realizing gains

Documenting these rules prevents impulsive trading decisions.

## Asset-location logic in plain language

A practical default:

- hold lower-turnover core positions in taxable when suitable
- keep higher-tax-drag assets in tax-advantaged space where possible
- perform most rebalancing inside sheltered accounts first

The objective is not “zero tax,” it is lower avoidable drag.

## Scenario: where should new money go?

You have $12,000 to deploy and two choices:

- add to taxable brokerage
- fill remaining tax-advantaged room first

Decision variables:

- expected holding period
- current marginal tax situation
- expected need for flexibility/liquidity

When in doubt, model after-tax outcomes over multiple years rather than this quarter alone.

## Rebalancing without tax surprises

Use a three-step sequence:

1. redirect new contributions to underweight assets
2. rebalance inside tax-advantaged accounts
3. realize taxable gains only when portfolio risk is materially off-target

This keeps allocation in range while minimizing avoidable taxable events.

## Loss-harvesting guardrails

Harvest only when all are true:

- replacement exposure is preselected
- wash-sale constraints are understood
- trade supports long-term allocation, not short-term anxiety

If the trade would not make sense without tax effects, pause.

## Annual review checklist

- verify realized gains/losses by quarter
- review unexpected fund distributions
- confirm account placements still match policy
- update thresholds as portfolio size and income change

## Keep records to simplify future decisions

Maintain a lightweight annual log of major trades, realized gains/losses, and rationale for location changes. This creates institutional memory for your own portfolio and reduces repeated mistakes when tax season or rebalancing decisions arrive.
