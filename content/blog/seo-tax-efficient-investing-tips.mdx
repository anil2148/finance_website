Many investors track pre-tax returns and ignore tax drag. Over decades, that drag can materially reduce what you keep.

## Focus on the three levers with the biggest impact

### 1) Asset location
Put tax-inefficient holdings in tax-advantaged accounts when possible, and reserve taxable accounts for lower-turnover, tax-efficient holdings.

### 2) Holding period discipline
Short-term gains are commonly taxed at higher rates than long-term gains. Frequent strategy shifts can create expensive tax noise.

### 3) Controlled turnover
Most portfolio improvements come from contribution consistency and allocation quality—not constant trading.

## Practical account-location framework

- **Taxable brokerage:** broad, low-turnover index exposure
- **Tax-deferred/tax-free accounts:** holdings that may produce higher ordinary-income tax drag
- **Rebalancing priority:** use tax-sheltered accounts first when possible

## Example tradeoff to model

Assume two choices for a new $12,000 annual contribution:

- Add to taxable account for flexibility
- Max tax-advantaged account first for potential tax benefit

The better decision depends on your current bracket, expected future bracket, and time-to-withdrawal. If you are unsure, model contribution scenarios in the [Retirement Calculator](/calculators/retirement-calculator) and compare account options in [Best Investment Apps](/best-investment-apps).

## Tax-loss harvesting: useful only with rules

Tax-loss harvesting can help, but ad-hoc harvesting often creates mistakes.

Set explicit rules:
- Define acceptable replacement funds in advance
- Track wash-sale risk windows
- Keep a written process so decisions are not headline-driven

## Common mistakes

- Optimizing taxes before fixing contribution consistency.
- Letting tax fear block needed risk control.
- Ignoring fund distributions in taxable accounts.
