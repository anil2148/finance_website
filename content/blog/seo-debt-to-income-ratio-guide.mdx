DTI improvement is not about one big payoff. It is about lowering required monthly obligations in the order that moves underwriting decisions.

## Step 1: classify your debts by underwriting impact

Create three buckets:

- **High impact:** debts with large required minimums (these move DTI fastest).
- **Medium impact:** installments where refinance could cut required payment.
- **Low impact:** balances with tiny minimums that barely move ratios.

Focus cash on high-impact items first.

## Step 2: build a 90-day calendar

### Days 1–15: stop profile damage
- No new credit applications.
- Pull current statements and verify reported balances.
- Document all income streams in one folder.

### Days 16–45: lower required payments
- Pay down balances producing the largest minimum payments.
- Evaluate refinance only if payment drops without extending debt into a harmful term.

### Days 46–90: keep the file clean
- Avoid unusual transfers that are hard to source.
- Keep balances stable before lender pulls updates.
- Respond to document requests within 24 hours.

## Numeric example: same salary, two outcomes

Gross income: $7,200/month.

| Scenario | Housing payment | Other required debt | Back-end DTI |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Before cleanup | $2,050 | $1,260 | 45.9% |
| After targeted paydown | $2,050 | $820 | 39.9% |

That change can move a file from borderline to comfortably approvable.

## Borrower decision test before submission

Submit only if all are true:

- You can explain every large deposit on statements.
- You can keep payments stable for the next two billing cycles.
- You are not relying on a temporary income spike to qualify.

## Borrower tradeoff decisions lenders notice

When borrowers try to cut DTI quickly, they often choose between two imperfect moves:

- paying off a card balance now, or
- keeping extra cash for reserves.

If your file is close to an underwriting threshold, reducing required monthly obligations usually has the bigger approval impact. If your file already clears DTI targets but reserves are thin, keeping liquidity can be smarter. Decide based on the bottleneck, not habit.
