DTI is not just a number lenders glance at. It is a signal of payment resilience. If you want better odds and cleaner pricing, improve DTI **before** rate shopping.

## Fast diagnosis: which DTI is your bottleneck?

- **Front-end DTI** = housing payment ÷ gross income.
- **Back-end DTI** = all required monthly debt ÷ gross income.

If front-end is fine but back-end is high, card and installment balances are usually the blocker.

## 90-day underwriting plan

### Days 1–14: remove preventable friction
- Pull your credit reports and match balances to current statements.
- Stop opening new credit.
- Document all income sources (especially overtime, bonus, or self-employment).

### Days 15–45: lower required obligations
- Prioritize revolving balances with high minimum payments.
- Consider refinancing only if it lowers required monthly payment and total cost is acceptable.
- Avoid replacing one high payment with multiple new obligations.

### Days 46–90: protect the file
- Keep utilization trends stable.
- Do not move money in ways that create documentation confusion.
- Build a clean packet of statements and pay records.

## Example: same salary, different outcome

Borrower income: $7,200/month gross.

Scenario A:
- Housing payment estimate: $2,050
- Other required debt: $1,250
- Back-end DTI: 45.8%

Scenario B after payoff/refinance actions:
- Housing payment estimate: $2,050
- Other required debt: $850
- Back-end DTI: 40.3%

That gap can materially improve approval confidence and pricing options.

## Decision checkpoints before you apply

1. Can you lower required payments without adding expensive long-term interest?
2. Are all balances and income sources easy to verify?
3. Is your payment behavior stable enough for the next 60 days?

If any answer is no, wait and tighten the profile first.

## Borrower tradeoff decisions lenders notice

When borrowers try to cut DTI quickly, they often choose between two imperfect moves:

- paying off a card balance now, or
- keeping extra cash for reserves.

If your file is close to an underwriting threshold, reducing required monthly obligations usually has the bigger approval impact. If your file already clears DTI targets but reserves are thin, keeping liquidity can be smarter. Decide based on the bottleneck, not habit.
