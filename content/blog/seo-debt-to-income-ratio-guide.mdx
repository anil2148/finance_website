## Front-end vs back-end DTI
Lenders usually review two versions:
- **Front-end DTI:** housing costs vs gross income.
- **Back-end DTI:** all monthly debt obligations vs gross income.

## Quick example
Gross monthly income: $6,500
- Housing payment: $1,850 → front-end DTI = 28%
- Total monthly debts: $2,650 → back-end DTI = 41%

A 41% back-end DTI can still qualify in some programs, but rates and conditions may be less favorable.

## Ways to improve DTI in 60–90 days
- Pay down revolving balances to lower minimum payments.
- Refinance one high-payment loan if total cost still improves.
- Avoid adding new installment debt before application.

## Tradeoff to understand
Stretching the loan term can lower DTI but raise total interest. Always compare qualification benefit vs lifetime cost.
