Debt-to-income (DTI) ratio answers one question for lenders: how much of your monthly gross income is already committed to debt payments?

## DTI formula and fast benchmark

DTI = total monthly debt payments ÷ gross monthly income

Example:

- Gross monthly income: $7,000
- Debt payments: $2,450

DTI = 35%

Lower DTI generally improves approval odds and borrowing terms.

## The 90-day DTI improvement plan

### Days 1–14: Identify high-impact levers

List debts by required monthly payment (not just balance). Extra payments that reduce minimums can improve DTI faster.

### Days 15–45: Target required payment reductions

- Refinance expensive installment debt where possible
- Pay down revolving balances to reduce minimums
- Remove authorized-user obligations if they hurt your profile

### Days 46–90: Stabilize and document

- Avoid taking new debt
- Maintain on-time payments
- Prepare income and liability documentation for underwriting

## Worked scenario

Starting point:

- Gross income: $6,500/month
- Debt payments: $2,730
- DTI: 42%

Actions:

- Credit card payoff lowers required payments by $180
- Personal loan refinance lowers payment by $120
- Car insurance savings redirected to debt payoff, reducing another $60 in obligations

New debt payments: $2,370

New DTI: 36.5%

That change can materially improve your loan options.

## What should you do now?

### Best option if your priority is mortgage approval

Prioritize reducing required monthly obligations and avoid new financing for 90 days before application.

### Best option if your priority is speed

Target revolving balances with the highest payment burden first. Use the [Credit Card Payoff Calculator](/calculators/credit-card-payoff-calculator).

### Best option if your priority is lower monthly stress

Refinance high-payment debt and extend term only when total interest tradeoff is acceptable.

## Common DTI mistakes

- Focusing only on debt balances instead of required monthly payments
- Applying for new credit right before a major loan application
- Ignoring documentation gaps that delay approvals
- Waiting until the month before home shopping to improve ratios
