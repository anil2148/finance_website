Improving your savings rate is usually easier with systems than with willpower. The goal is not to cut everything—it is to build repeatable levers that move your monthly transfer amount up over time.

## Three levers that usually work

1. **Automation first:** raise automatic transfers each time income increases.
2. **One fixed + one variable cut:** trim one recurring bill and one flexible category.
3. **Windfall capture:** route bonuses, refunds, or side-income directly into savings.

## A practical example

If you increase monthly savings by just $150, that is $1,800 per year before investment growth. Stack two small wins and the yearly impact compounds quickly.

## Keep it sustainable

Use changes you can maintain for at least 6 months. Extreme cuts often rebound and erase progress.

## 90-day implementation plan

Week 1: set your baseline savings rate.

Week 2: implement one fixed-expense cut.

Week 3: automate a higher transfer.

Weeks 4-12: protect that system and avoid lifestyle creep.

Small improvements repeated across 90 days create durable progress without dramatic short-term restrictions.

Track this with one number: monthly transfer rate. If that number trends up, your system is working.
