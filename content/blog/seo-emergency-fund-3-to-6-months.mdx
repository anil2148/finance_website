“Three to six months” is too broad to act on. You need a target tied to your risk profile and recovery timeline.

## Build your target with a weighted score

Score each item:

- Variable income: **+2**
- Single-income household: **+2**
- Dependents with fixed obligations: **+1**
- Industry with frequent layoffs/cycles: **+2**
- High unavoidable health/transport cost risk: **+1**

### Interpreting your score

- **0–2:** 3 months of essentials
- **3–5:** 4–5 months
- **6–8:** 6–8 months

## Worked example

Household essentials: $4,100/month.

Risk profile:
- one earner (+2)
- commission-heavy income (+2)
- two dependents (+1)
- cyclical industry (+2)

Total score = **7** → target range: **6–8 months**.

Initial target at 6 months = **$24,600**.

## Funding plan that avoids burnout

1. **Stage 1:** first $2,000 for immediate shocks.
2. **Stage 2:** one full essentials month ($4,100).
3. **Stage 3:** finish long-term reserve with automatic transfers and irregular-income sweeps.

Use two lanes:
- Base lane = fixed transfer every payday.
- Variable lane = 40%–60% of bonus/refund/side-income deposits.
