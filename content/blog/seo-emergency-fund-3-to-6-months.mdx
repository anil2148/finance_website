## Use a risk-based target, not a generic rule
The 3–6 month range is a starting point. Your real number depends on:
- income stability,
- number of dependents,
- fixed monthly obligations,
- how quickly you could replace income.

## Practical targeting framework
- **3 months:** stable income, low fixed costs, strong backup support.
- **4–5 months:** moderate job volatility or single-income household.
- **6+ months:** variable income, high fixed expenses, or specialized career transition risk.

## Build in phases
1. Starter reserve: $1,000–$2,000.
2. Core reserve: one month of essentials.
3. Full reserve: your personalized target.

This phased approach improves consistency and confidence.
