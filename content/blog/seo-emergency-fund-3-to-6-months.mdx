“Three to six months” is a useful slogan, but it is not a decision. Households with identical expenses can need very different reserve sizes because recovery time is different.

Use this method to set a target that reflects your real risk, not a generic rule.

## Step 1: score interruption risk

Add points for each condition that is true **today**:

- Variable or commission-heavy pay: **+2**
- Single-income household: **+2**
- Dependents with fixed commitments: **+1**
- Industry with clear layoff cycles: **+2**
- Limited family support network: **+1**
- High unavoidable health/transport exposure: **+1**

### Reserve band from score

- **0–2 points:** 3 months essentials
- **3–5 points:** 4–5 months essentials
- **6–9 points:** 6–8 months essentials

## Step 2: define essentials correctly

Most people either overstate or understate “essentials.” Use only bills that protect housing, food, utilities, transport, and minimum debt obligations.

Exclude discretionary subscriptions, upgrades, and optional travel spending.

## Step 3: choose a staged funding plan

Example household:

- Essentials: **$4,300/month**
- Risk score: **6 points**
- Initial target band: **6–8 months**
- First full target: **$25,800** (6 months)

Funding stages:

1. **Shock buffer:** first $2,000 for immediate emergencies.
2. **Breathing room:** one essentials month ($4,300).
3. **Core reserve:** build to three months.
4. **Full resilience:** finish six-month target.

This sequencing lowers burnout and keeps motivation visible.

## Decision point: pay debt faster or finish reserves first?

Use this tie-breaker:

- If your top APR is very high and stable income is strong, run a blended plan.
- If your income is variable or job risk is elevated, reserve depth usually comes first.

In practice, many households do best with a 70/30 split (reserve/debt) until the first 1–2 months of essentials are complete.

## Account structure that prevents accidental spending

- Keep **one month** in fastest-access cash.
- Keep remaining reserve in a separate HYSA bucket.
- Turn on alerts for large withdrawals and low balance thresholds.
