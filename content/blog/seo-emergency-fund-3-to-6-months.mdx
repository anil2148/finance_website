## Who this is for
This guide is for people who want a financial cushion before the next unexpected bill hits, especially if you have little to no savings today.

## Why this matters right now
Recent US survey data shows emergency savings are still fragile in 2026:
- Only **46%** of Americans report having enough savings to cover three months of expenses.
- **24%** report having no emergency savings.
- **43%** say they could not cover a **$1,000** surprise expense from savings alone.

That means many households are still one interruption away from debt.

## How much should you save?
A useful target is based on **essential monthly expenses** (housing, food, utilities, minimum debt payments, and transportation).

| Situation | Recommended target |
| --- | --- |
| Stable job + dual income | 3 months of essentials |
| Single income or variable pay | 6 months of essentials |
| Self-employed / freelance | 9–12 months of essentials |
| High fixed obligations | 6+ months |

## Where to keep your emergency fund
Prioritize three things: safety, access in 1–2 business days, and yield.

In early 2026, many online HYSAs offer roughly **4.5% to 5.0% APY**, far above typical brick-and-mortar savings yields.

Good options:
- High-yield savings account (HYSA)
- Money market account
- Short-term CD ladder for a portion of funds

Usually avoid:
- Brokerage accounts (market risk)
- Retirement accounts (tax/penalty risk)
- Checking accounts (spending leakage)

## Key tradeoffs
A larger emergency fund gives safety but may slow down investing. The right balance depends on job stability, debt load, and fixed monthly obligations.

## Example scenario
If your essential expenses are **$3,200/month**, your emergency target is:
- 3 months: **$9,600**
- 6 months: **$19,200**

At a 5.00% APY HYSA, automating **$400/month** gets you to the 3-month target in about 24 months.

## Common mistakes
- Underestimating essentials by counting only rent or mortgage
- Keeping emergency money in checking and spending it accidentally
- Using the fund for non-emergencies (holidays, discretionary shopping)
- Not updating your target when living costs rise

## Next steps
1. Calculate your essential monthly expenses.
2. Set an initial 3-month target.
3. Compare HYSA options with no monthly fee.
4. Automate transfers (even $50–$100/month to start).
5. Review and adjust yearly.

An emergency fund is a risk-management tool, not a performance tool. Build it in layers, keep it liquid, and automate contributions so progress continues even during busy months.
