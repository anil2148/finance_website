Some investors can realize long-term capital gains at **0% federal tax** in lower-income years. If used carefully, this can reset cost basis and lower lifetime tax drag without changing your portfolio goals.

## The 0% long-term capital-gains bracket

Long-term capital gains use different federal brackets than ordinary income.

In 2026, the 0% band is generally available only up to certain taxable-income limits (roughly in the **low-to-mid $50k range for single filers** and about **double that for married filing jointly**, subject to final IRS figures).

## When to realize gains

0% harvesting is often strongest in:

- low-income years,
- gap years between jobs,
- early retirement before Social Security/RMDs,
- sabbaticals with reduced earned income.

Always check side effects such as ACA premium-credit impacts and other income-based benefits before executing large sales.

## Timing strategy

- Harvest gains up to your estimated 0% headroom.
- Spread large multi-position gains across multiple years.
- Avoid stacking all gains into already high-income years.

Example: a **$20,000 long-term gain** realized in a 0% year can cost **$0 federal** versus about **$3,000** at a 15% rate in a future high-income year.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting state capital-gains tax.
- Realizing too much and accidentally spilling into higher federal rates.
- Ignoring interactions with benefits and credits tied to income.

## Implementation checklist

1. Estimate this year’s taxable income.
2. Calculate 0% capital-gains headroom.
3. Select positions to sell based on your portfolio plan.
4. Realize gains up to planned headroom.
5. Document new cost basis for future planning.
