Preapproval is mostly a documentation and consistency exercise. Borrowers are often surprised that approvals slow down because of missing pages, unexplained transfers, or debt changes during review.

## Documents lenders usually ask for
Gather these before you apply:

- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- Last 2 years of W-2s (or 1099/tax returns for self-employment)
- Most recent bank and investment statements (all pages)
- Government ID and proof of address
- Current debt obligations and minimum payments
- Gift letter and paper trail (if down payment help is involved)

If your income varies, expect additional requests (year-to-date profit/loss, contracts, or business returns).

## Typical timeline (when paperwork is clean)
- **Days 1–2:** Application + credit pull
- **Days 2–5:** Initial underwriting review + document clarifications
- **Days 5–10:** Conditional preapproval decision

Any missing document or inconsistent number can reset portions of this timeline.

## Qualification factors that move your rate and approval odds
- Credit profile and recent delinquencies
- Debt-to-income ratio
- Loan-to-value and down payment source
- Employment stability and income history

If one area is weaker, a stronger down payment or lower DTI can sometimes offset risk.

## Common blockers
- Large unexplained deposits close to application date
- Opening new credit lines while underwriting is in process
- Incomplete statement PDFs (missing blank pages)
- Frequent account transfers without clear purpose
