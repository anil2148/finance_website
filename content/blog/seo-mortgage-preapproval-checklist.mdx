Mortgage preapproval is mostly a **documentation accuracy** process. Borrowers lose time when statements are incomplete, deposits are unclear, or debt changes mid-review.

## Document package to prepare before applying

- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- Last 2 years of W-2s (or 1099s/tax returns for self-employment)
- Recent bank/investment statements (all pages, including blanks)
- Government ID and address proof
- Current debt obligations and minimum payments
- Gift-letter documentation if down payment funds are gifted

If income is variable, expect additional requests (YTD P&L, contracts, business returns).

## Typical timeline when paperwork is clean

1. **Days 1-2:** application and credit pull
2. **Days 2-5:** underwriting review + clarification requests
3. **Days 5-10:** conditional preapproval decision

Inconsistent numbers, missing pages, or unexplained transfers can reset parts of the timeline.

## Qualification factors that change both approval and pricing

- Credit profile and recent payment history
- Debt-to-income ratio
- Down payment size and source quality
- Employment/income consistency

Use the [Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage-calculator) to test payment ranges before shopping.

## Common delays you can avoid

- Large unexplained deposits right before application
- Opening new credit lines during underwriting
- Sending cropped statement screenshots instead of full PDFs
- Frequent money movement without clear paper trail
