## What preapproval actually does
Preapproval is not a final loan commitment, but it gives you a lender-verified borrowing range and flags issues early. In competitive markets, it also strengthens your purchase offer.

## Document checklist before you apply
Prepare these upfront to reduce underwriting back-and-forth:
- Government-issued ID
- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- Last 2 years of W-2s or tax returns (especially for variable income)
- 2-3 months of bank statements
- Current debt statements (student loans, auto loans, cards)
- Employment details and recent address history

Self-employed borrowers often need additional profit-and-loss and business-return documentation.

## Typical timeline (if your file is clean)
1. **Day 1-2:** Initial application and soft pull or hard pull (lender dependent).
2. **Day 2-5:** Document verification and conditional preapproval.
3. **Day 5-10:** Clarification requests or updated documentation.
4. **After accepted offer:** Full underwriting and appraisal.

Delays usually come from missing income documentation, unexplained large deposits, or debt changes during review.

## Lender requirements to watch closely
Different lenders have different tolerances, but most pay attention to:
- debt-to-income ratio,
- credit history and recent inquiries,
- reserve assets after down payment,
- stability of income source.

Do not open new credit cards or finance a car while your mortgage file is active.

## Cost tradeoffs: rate, points, and monthly payment
Compare offers on **all-in cost**, not headline rate alone.

A loan with a slightly lower rate but high upfront points may be worse if you plan to move or refinance in a few years. Ask each lender for:
- APR,
- total cash to close,
- monthly payment estimate,
- break-even timeline on discount points.

## Common preapproval delays and fixes
- **Issue:** Inconsistent income docs. **Fix:** provide matching pay periods and complete statements.
- **Issue:** Unverified deposits. **Fix:** provide source documentation early.
- **Issue:** Rising card balances. **Fix:** keep utilization low before and during underwriting.
