Preapproval is less about getting a number and more about reducing deal risk before you start making offers.

## The 4-bucket preapproval checklist

### 1) Income verification

- Recent pay stubs
- W-2s or 1099 history
- Bonus/commission documentation if relevant

### 2) Asset verification

- Bank statements for down payment and reserves
- Retirement account snapshots if used for reserve strength
- Gift letter documentation if receiving family funds

### 3) Liability profile

- Current debt payment list
- Minimum-payment details
- Any recently opened credit accounts

### 4) Property-range plan

- Target monthly payment comfort zone
- Estimated taxes/insurance/HOA assumptions
- Down payment percentage and closing-cost buffer

## Run affordability before lender conversations

Example decision framework:

- Gross household income: $11,000/month
- Target all-in housing payment cap: 28% to 32% gross ($3,080 to $3,520)
- Keep post-close cash reserve: at least 3 months of survival expenses

Use the [Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage-calculator) for payment ranges and [Net Worth Calculator](/net-worth-calculator) for post-close liquidity checks.

## Lender comparison strategy

Get at least three preapproval options within a short window and compare:

- Rate and APR
- Points/credits structure
- Estimated lender fees
- Underwriting timeline confidence

Rate alone is not enough; execution quality matters in competitive markets.

Compare options via [Mortgage Rate Comparison](/mortgage-rate-comparison) and broader alternatives on [Finance Product Comparison](/comparison).

## What should you do now?

### Best option if you are buying in 60–90 days

Complete document prep first, then request preapprovals in a tight 7-day window.

### Best option if your priority is maximum approval amount

Improve DTI before applying by reducing required monthly obligations.

### Best option if your priority is lower stress

Set a purchase budget below maximum approval so unexpected taxes/insurance changes do not break your plan.

## Mistakes that delay closings

- Moving large unexplained deposits into accounts during underwriting
- Changing jobs or compensation structure mid-process without planning
- Opening new credit cards before closing
- Choosing a home price based on max approval instead of payment comfort

## Decision Playbook

### What should you do now
1. Run your current numbers in the [recommended calculator](/calculators/mortgage-calculator) and write down your baseline monthly result.
2. Pick one high-impact change and test a 90-day target. Example: shifting **$938/month** to **$536/month** frees about **$4,824/year**.
3. Compare one alternative on a side-by-side page before committing: [comparison guide](/compare/mortgage-rate-comparison).

### Best option for beginners
For most beginners, start with a simple automatic rule: route **$1206/month** to the goal first, then spend the rest. At a 7% annualized return, **$1206/month** for 10 years can grow to roughly **$208,638** (contribution + growth estimate).

### If your income is $X → do this
- **If take-home income is under $6,700/month:** prioritize stability, keep fixed costs below ~60%, and automate at least **$670/month**.
- **If take-home income is $6,700–$8,500/month:** automate **15%–20%** and add a quarterly increase of **1% of pay**.
- **If take-home income is above $8,500/month:** front-load goals with at least **25% savings/investing** and avoid lifestyle creep.

### If your goal is Y → choose this
If your goal is **buy a home in 2 years**, choose the option that gives the strongest tradeoff between cash-flow flexibility and total long-term cost. Use this sequence: calculator result → comparison page → one-month pilot → 12-month commitment.

### What to avoid
- Waiting for a “perfect” plan instead of starting with measurable numbers this month.
- Making multiple major changes at once (hard to diagnose what worked).
- Ignoring internal benchmarks and related playbooks: [related guide 1](/blog/debt-to-income-ratio-90-day-plan) and [related guide 2](/blog/mortgage-preapproval-checklist-underwriting).
