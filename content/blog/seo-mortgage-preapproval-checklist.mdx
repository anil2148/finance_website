Preapproval speed is mostly documentation quality. Even qualified borrowers get stuck when statements, deposits, and liabilities are unclear.

## One-page preapproval prep board

### A) Income packet
- Last 30 days of pay stubs
- Two years of W-2s (or full self-employment package)
- Written explanation for variable compensation when applicable

### B) Asset packet
- Full bank statements (all pages)
- Investment statements if funds are used for close/reserves
- Source trail for any large non-payroll deposits

### C) Liability packet
- Current debt list with minimum payments
- ID and address documentation

## Timeline when file quality is strong

- **Day 1:** application + credit pull
- **Days 2–4:** initial underwriting review
- **Days 5–8:** conditional items and clarifications
- **Days 9–12:** preapproval issuance when conditions are satisfied

## Delay triggers to eliminate now

- Missing pages from PDF statements
- New credit activity during review
- Unexplained transfers between accounts
- Screenshot evidence instead of official statements

## Scenario: faster file vs slower file

Two borrowers with similar income and credit profiles submit applications.

- Borrower A provides complete PDFs and sourced deposits upfront.
- Borrower B provides partial screenshots and undocumented transfers.

Borrower A often gets fewer conditions and quicker turnaround despite similar risk profile.
