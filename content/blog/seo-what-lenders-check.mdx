## Underwriting is about consistency
Lenders verify whether your income, debt, and assets support repayment under policy thresholds. Small inconsistencies can slow or derail approval.

## Core areas lenders review
- Credit history and recent inquiries
- Income stability and documentation quality
- Debt-to-income ratio and payment obligations
- Cash reserves and source of funds
- Employment and identity verification

## Borrower readiness checklist
1. Pull credit reports and fix obvious errors.
2. Organize income and asset docs before applying.
3. Avoid new debt accounts during underwriting.
4. Keep deposit activity explainable.

## Example risk case
A borrower with strong income but high revolving utilization may still face stricter terms. Lowering balances before application can improve approval profile.
