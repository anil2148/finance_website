## Why travel cards matter in 2026

Travel credit cards can unlock valuable points, miles, and perks like trip protections and lounge access when you use them for everyday spending and travel purchases.[web:71][web:74] In 2026, competition is strong, so many issuers offer big welcome bonuses and rich category bonuses on travel, dining, and online purchases.[web:71][web:80]

The right travel card can meaningfully reduce the cost of flights and hotels over time, but only if you pick one that matches how often and how you travel.[web:71][web:74]

## How to choose the right travel card

1. **Estimate your travel frequency and spend.** Frequent travelers can justify higher annual fees; occasional travelers usually do better with low-fee or flexible options.[web:71][web:74]
2. **Decide between flexible points and loyalty.** Flexible currencies (like Chase or Capital One) suit general travelers; co-branded airline/hotel cards suit brand loyalists.[web:71][web:74]
3. **Compare total value.** Look at welcome bonuses, ongoing rewards, travel credits, and perks like insurance and lounge access versus the annual fee.[web:71][web:80]
4. **Avoid interest.** Travel rewards make sense only if you pay in full each month and avoid carrying high-interest balances.[web:74]

## Action plan

- Start with one flexible travel card that fits your budget and travel patterns.  
- Add a co-branded airline or hotel card later only if you frequently fly or stay with that brand.  
- Use the card for travel and everyday bonus categories, then track redemptions toward your next trip.
