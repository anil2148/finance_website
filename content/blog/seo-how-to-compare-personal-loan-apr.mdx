## APR is necessary, not sufficient
APR standardizes borrowing cost, but offer quality also depends on origination fees, term length, prepayment policy, and funding reliability.

## True-cost checklist
1. Net proceeds after fees.
2. Total repayment across full term.
3. Optional early payoff flexibility.
4. Monthly payment under income stress (for example -10%).

## Worked comparison
- Offer A: 10.9% APR, 36 months, 4% origination fee.
- Offer B: 11.6% APR, 24 months, no fee.
Offer B may produce lower total interest despite higher APR because term is shorter and fees are lower.

## Decision tradeoffs
- Need lower monthly payment now? longer term helps cash flow.
- Want lowest total borrowing cost? shorter term generally wins.
