The best personal loan is not always the lowest APR ad. It is the offer you can carry through a rough quarter without default pressure.

## Compare offers in this sequence

1. **Net proceeds today** (loan amount minus upfront fees).
2. **Monthly payment under stress** (simulate a 10% income dip).
3. **Total repayment** over full term.
4. **Prepayment behavior** (extra payments to principal, no hidden friction).
5. **Servicer quality** (payment posting and support reliability).

## Offer worksheet example

Need: $18,000 for a planned expense.

| Offer | APR | Term | Origination | Net proceeds | Monthly payment tendency |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| A | 10.9% | 36 mo | 4% | Lower net cash | Lower payment, longer exposure |
| B | 11.6% | 24 mo | 0% | Full net cash | Higher payment, lower total interest |

Offer B can win if cash flow can support the higher payment consistently.

## Hard yes / no rules before signing

Approve only if all are true:

- Payment still works after a stress test.
- Total repayment aligns with your debt-free timeline.
- Terms for extra payments are clearly documented.
- You received complete, written disclosures.

If one fails, keep shopping.
