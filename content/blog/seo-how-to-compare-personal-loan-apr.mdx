## Who this is for
People comparing personal loan offers who want to avoid misleading “low-rate” quotes.

## Why APR matters more than interest rate
Interest rate reflects borrowing cost on principal. **APR** is broader: it annualizes borrowing cost including required fees.

Two loans can share the same interest rate but have different APRs if one charges a high origination fee.

## 2026 rate context
In March 2026, many borrower-facing personal loan quotes still span a wide range (single digits for top-tier profiles to very high double digits for riskier profiles). That makes apples-to-apples APR comparison essential.

## How to compare offers correctly
1. Ask each lender for the **APR**, not just note rate.
2. Confirm whether origination fee is deducted from proceeds.
3. Compute total paid: `(monthly payment × term months) + upfront fees`.
4. Compare offers by lender type (bank, credit union, online lender).
5. Check for prepayment penalties.

## Example scenario
You request $10,000:
- Offer A: 10.5% rate, 5% origination fee (you receive $9,500)
- Offer B: 11.2% rate, 0% origination fee (you receive $10,000)

Offer A may look cheaper on rate but can be more expensive once fee-adjusted APR and net proceeds are considered.

## Key tradeoffs
- Lowest advertised rate is not always lowest total cost.
- No-fee loans may have slightly higher rates but lower all-in cost.
- Longer terms reduce monthly payment but can increase total paid.

## Common mistakes
- Comparing quotes where one includes fees and another does not
- Taking first offer without rate-shopping
- Ignoring prepayment terms and payoff flexibility
