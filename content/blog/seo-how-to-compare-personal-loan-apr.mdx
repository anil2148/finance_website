The best personal loan is not always the lowest APR ad. It is the offer you can carry through a rough quarter without default pressure.

## Compare offers in this sequence

1. **Net proceeds today** (loan amount minus upfront fees).
2. **Monthly payment under stress** (simulate a 10% income dip).
3. **Total repayment** over full term.
4. **Prepayment behavior** (extra payments to principal, no hidden friction).
5. **Servicer quality** (payment posting and support reliability).

## Offer worksheet example

Need: $18,000 for a planned expense.

| Offer | APR | Term | Origination | Net proceeds | Monthly payment tendency |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| A | 10.9% | 36 mo | 4% | Lower net cash | Lower payment, longer exposure |
| B | 11.6% | 24 mo | 0% | Full net cash | Higher payment, lower total interest |

Offer B can win if cash flow can support the higher payment consistently.

## Hard yes / no rules before signing

Approve only if all are true:

- Payment still works after a stress test.
- Total repayment aligns with your debt-free timeline.
- Terms for extra payments are clearly documented.
- You received complete, written disclosures.

If one fails, keep shopping.

- Monthly payment stays manageable after stress test.
- Fee structure is transparent and written clearly.
- Prepayment terms are borrower-friendly.
- Total repayment aligns with your debt-reduction timeline.

If one criterion fails, keep shopping.

## One more stress test before accepting

Run a “bad quarter” simulation:

- income drops by 10%,
- one irregular expense hits (car, medical, home repair),
- and your payment must still clear without new debt.

If the loan only works in a perfect month, it is priced too aggressively for your risk profile even when APR looks competitive.

## Red flags that justify walking away

- Verbal fee promises that do not match disclosure documents.
- Pressure to sign before you receive a full amortization schedule.
- Servicer reputation problems around payment posting or payoff processing.

If the documentation is inconsistent, the loan is already too risky.

## Pre-signing checklist

- Confirm first payment date and grace details.
- Confirm how extra payments are applied.
- Confirm whether autopay changes APR and if that rate is revocable.
- Save all final documents in one folder before funding.
