APR is important, but not sufficient. Borrowers often choose the "lowest APR" offer and later discover higher all-in cost or poor repayment flexibility.

## The 5 checks that prevent bad loan decisions

1. **Net amount received after fees** (origination/admin fees reduce usable cash).
2. **Total repayment over full term** (principal + interest + fees).
3. **Payment under stress** (e.g., temporary 10% income drop).
4. **Prepayment terms** (is extra payment applied to principal without penalties?).
5. **Funding reliability** (actual disbursement time and lender consistency).

## Worked example: similar APR, different outcomes

Need: **$15,000**

- **Offer A:** 10.9% APR, 36 months, 4% origination fee
- **Offer B:** 11.6% APR, 24 months, no origination fee

Offer A may advertise a lower APR, but fee drag and longer term can raise total cost materially. Offer B can be cheaper overall if the monthly payment is manageable.

Test both scenarios in the [Loan Calculator](/calculators/loan-calculator).

## Match loan structure to borrower reality

- **Cash-flow tight today:** longer term may be necessary, but recognize interest tradeoff.
- **Stable income, cost-focused:** shorter term often reduces total interest.
- **Variable/commission income:** prioritize payment flexibility and emergency reserves.

## Red flags worth walking away from

- "Guaranteed approval" before basic underwriting.
- Fee language that is vague or inconsistent across disclosures.
- Pressure to sign immediately without a full amortization review.
