APR is only one column in the decision. The winning offer is the one with acceptable monthly pressure and the lowest realistic all-in cost.

## Evaluate offers in this order

### 1) Net cash received
Origination fees can reduce usable funds immediately.

### 2) Monthly payment under stress
Can you still pay if income drops 10% for one quarter?

### 3) Total repayment
Shorter terms often cost less overall but demand stronger monthly cash flow.

### 4) Prepayment behavior
Verify extra payments reduce principal without hidden restrictions.

### 5) Lender execution quality
How consistent are funding timelines and servicing support?

## Side-by-side example

Borrower needs $15,000 for a defined expense.

- Offer A: 10.8% APR, 36 months, 4% origination
- Offer B: 11.5% APR, 24 months, no origination

Offer A may advertise the prettier APR, while Offer B can produce lower total cost if monthly payment remains safe in your budget.

Test both in the [Loan Calculator](/calculators/loan-calculator).

## Decision matrix

Choose the offer that meets **all** of these:

- Monthly payment stays manageable after stress test.
- Fee structure is transparent and written clearly.
- Prepayment terms are borrower-friendly.
- Total repayment aligns with your debt-reduction timeline.

If one criterion fails, keep shopping.

## Red flags that justify walking away

- Verbal fee promises that do not match disclosure documents.
- Pressure to sign before you receive a full amortization schedule.
- Servicer reputation problems around payment posting or payoff processing.

If the documentation is inconsistent, the loan is already too risky.

## Pre-signing checklist

- Confirm first payment date and grace details.
- Confirm how extra payments are applied.
- Confirm whether autopay changes APR and if that rate is revocable.
- Save all final documents in one folder before funding.
