## APR is the starting point, not the full answer
APR helps normalize cost, but two similar APR loans can have very different real-world outcomes due to fees and term structure.

## Four checks before accepting an offer
1. Origination fee impact on net proceeds.
2. Prepayment flexibility (penalty or no penalty).
3. Late-fee and grace-period terms.
4. Monthly payment under a stress scenario (for example, 10% lower income).

## Example
Loan A: lower APR, longer term.
Loan B: slightly higher APR, shorter term and lower total interest.
Choose based on whether payment stability or total interest minimization is your primary objective.
