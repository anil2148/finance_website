## Start with how you actually invest
Choose the product that supports your behavior:
- If you want automatic paycheck investing, index mutual funds are often easier.
- If you want intraday flexibility and precise tax-lot control, ETFs may fit better.

## Key tradeoffs
- **Taxes (taxable accounts):** ETFs often have an edge.
- **Automation:** index funds often win for simplicity.
- **Minimums:** many ETFs allow fractional shares; some index funds require minimums.
- **Behavior risk:** intraday ETF trading can encourage overtrading.

## Example decision framework
- **401(k)/IRA beginner:** prioritize low-cost simplicity.
- **Taxable long-term investor:** prioritize tax efficiency and bid-ask awareness.
- **Hands-on optimizer:** can combine both intentionally.

## Common mistakes
- Picking based only on expense ratio.
- Ignoring spread/slippage for thinly traded ETFs.
- Chasing niche funds that duplicate core exposure.
