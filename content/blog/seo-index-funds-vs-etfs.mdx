## They can hold the same assets but behave differently
Index mutual funds and ETFs may track similar indexes, but the investor experience differs on automation, trading, tax mechanics, and minimums.

## Where index funds often win
- Simple automatic investing from paycheck cadence.
- Fractional contributions without trading decisions.
- Easy set-and-forget behavior for long horizons.

## Where ETFs often win
- Intraday trading flexibility.
- Usually lower minimums at many brokerages.
- Potential tax advantages in some structures.

## Behavior tradeoff
ETF flexibility can tempt overtrading. If that increases reaction to headlines, lower fees may be offset by worse decisions.

## Scenario
If you invest monthly and never trade intraday, either structure can work. Pick the one with better automation and lower all-in cost on your platform.
