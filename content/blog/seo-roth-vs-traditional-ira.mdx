## Use tax rate comparison, not internet myths
The best choice usually depends on your current marginal bracket versus expected retirement bracket, plus your need for flexibility.

## Decision anchors
- **Roth IRA:** pay taxes now, qualified withdrawals tax-free later.
- **Traditional IRA:** potential tax deduction now, taxable withdrawals later.

## Practical scenario
If you are early-career and likely in a lower bracket today, Roth often provides cleaner long-term upside. If you are in a peak earning year, Traditional contributions may deliver stronger current-year tax relief.

## Flexibility considerations
Roth contribution basis is generally more accessible, which can matter for uncertain medium-term goals. Traditional may require stricter planning around withdrawal timing and tax impact.
