## Why small fees become big money
A 0.70% fee gap can look trivial monthly, but over decades it can mean tens of thousands of dollars in missed growth.

If you invest $500/month for 25 years at 7% gross return:
- Portfolio at 0.08% fees can finish materially higher.
- Portfolio at 0.85% total costs can lag significantly.

## What to check beyond the headline expense ratio
- Fund expense ratio
- Platform/account fees
- Advisory fee (if applicable)
- Trading spreads and tax drag in taxable accounts

Use "all-in cost" as your comparison metric.

## Practical fee benchmarks
These aren’t hard rules, but useful flags:
- Broad index ETFs: often very low cost
- Target-date funds: moderate cost range
- Actively managed thematic funds: often much higher

High fees need a strong, evidence-based reason.

## Mistakes investors make
- Comparing only one fund instead of total account costs.
- Ignoring taxes while optimizing fees.
- Switching funds too often and creating avoidable costs.
