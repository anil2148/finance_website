## Small percentages create large long-term drag
Expense ratios look tiny, but they reduce return every year. Over decades, compounding makes fee differences meaningful.

## Quick cost example
Portfolio: $50,000, 25-year horizon, 7% gross return.
- Fund X fee: 0.05%
- Fund Y fee: 0.85%
The higher-cost option can reduce ending value by tens of thousands depending on contribution pace.

## What to compare beyond headline fee
- Fund expense ratio
- Trading spread or commission costs
- Advisory/platform fees
- Tax efficiency in taxable accounts

## Reasonable ranges (general)
- Broad index ETFs/funds: often very low cost
- Thematic or actively managed funds: typically higher
Higher fees are only justified when strategy value is clear and persistent.
