## The decision is mostly behavioral, not mathematical
Historically, lump sum often has higher expected return because money is invested earlier. But many investors abandon lump-sum plans during volatility. If DCA keeps you invested, it can be the better real-world choice.

## When DCA is usually the better fit
- You’re anxious about short-term losses.
- The cash came from a windfall and feels hard to deploy at once.
- You need a rule to avoid emotional market timing.

Example: invest $60,000 over 10 months at $6,000/month with an automatic schedule.

## When lump sum may be better
- You already hold a diversified long-term strategy.
- Short-term drawdowns won’t change your plan.
- You can tolerate seeing an immediate decline without panic selling.

## Hybrid strategy that works for many investors
Use a split approach:
- Invest 50% immediately.
- DCA the remaining 50% over 6 months.
This lowers regret risk while avoiding excessive cash drag.

## Mistakes to avoid
- Changing DCA cadence based on headlines.
- Stopping contributions after one down month.
- Holding too much cash "temporarily" for years.
