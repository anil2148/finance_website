## DCA vs lump sum: pick based on behavior risk
Historically, lump sum often wins mathematically in rising markets. But dollar-cost averaging (DCA) can win behaviorally if it keeps you consistently invested.

## Scenario comparison
You have $24,000 to invest:
- **Lump sum:** all at once now.
- **DCA:** $2,000 monthly for 12 months.

If markets fall early, DCA may reduce regret. If markets rise steadily, lump sum usually captures more growth sooner.

## When DCA is the better choice
- You fear investing a large amount at once.
- Cash flow is monthly by nature.
- You need a rules-based entry plan.

## When lump sum is reasonable
- You already have emergency reserves.
- You can tolerate volatility without stopping contributions.
- You are investing for 10+ years.
