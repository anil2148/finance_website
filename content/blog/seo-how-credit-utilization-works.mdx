Utilization is not one number. Lenders and scoring models react to **pattern quality**: overall usage, single-card stress, and repeat behavior.

## Your monthly utilization dashboard

Track these three metrics every month:

- Overall utilization across all revolving accounts
- Highest individual-card utilization
- Number of cards reporting a balance

A profile with one card reporting 70% can underperform even when total utilization looks fine.

## 30-day operating cycle

### Day 1–5: map statement closes
List close date and due date for each card.

### Day 6–20: pre-close payments
Push high-utilization cards below 30% before statement close.

### Day 21–30: concentration fix
Move recurring charges from your most-utilized card to lower-utilization cards.

## Real example

- Card A: limit $2,000, balance $1,100 (55%)
- Card B: limit $9,000, balance $900 (10%)
- Overall utilization: 18.2%

After two cycles:
- Card A reports $420 (21%)
- Card B reports $1,050 (11.7%)
- Overall utilization: 13.4%

Score momentum improves because both concentration and total usage improved.

## Avoid these common mistakes

- Closing old no-fee cards that support available credit.
- Carrying interest believing it “builds” score.
- Applying for new cards right before large loan shopping.

## Decision: pay one card hard or spread payments?

If one card is above 50% utilization, concentrated payoff often improves score momentum faster than evenly spreading extra cash across all cards. Once the highest-utilization card drops below ~30%, shift to broader paydown. This “de-concentrate first” approach usually outperforms random balance reductions.
