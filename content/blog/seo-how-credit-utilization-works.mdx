Credit utilization is not just “total balances divided by total limits.” Scoring systems can also penalize concentration risk when one card is near maxed out—even if your overall percentage looks fine.

## Two utilization metrics to track
- **Overall utilization:** total revolving balances ÷ total limits
- **Per-card utilization:** balance ÷ limit for each card

Healthy ranges are context-dependent, but most borrowers see better outcomes by keeping both metrics controlled.

## Timing tactics that matter
### 1) Pay before statement close
Your statement balance is often what gets reported. Paying 3–5 days before close date can reduce reported utilization.

### 2) Split payments during the month
If spend is high on one card, make a mid-cycle payment to avoid a high reported ratio.

### 3) Request limit increases strategically
A limit increase can improve utilization if spending stays flat—but only request when income and profile support approval odds.

## Example
- Card A: $1,000 limit, $800 balance → 80%
- Card B: $9,000 limit, $900 balance → 10%
- Overall utilization: 17%

Even with a reasonable overall figure, Card A can still drag score trends because utilization is concentrated.

## Mistakes to avoid
- Closing old no-fee cards that support total available credit
- Carrying interest based on the myth that it helps score
- Opening multiple new cards quickly just to “game” utilization
