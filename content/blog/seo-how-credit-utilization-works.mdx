## Credit utilization in plain language
Credit utilization is the percentage of your available revolving credit that you are currently using.

Formula:
**current balance ÷ total credit limit = utilization rate**

If you owe $1,200 across cards with a combined $6,000 limit, utilization is 20%.

## Why timing matters more than people think
Many people pay in full every month and still report high utilization because the statement closes before payment posts.

That means your score can temporarily drop even when you are financially responsible.

## Practical targets
There is no single perfect number, but many borrowers see better outcomes when they keep:
- overall utilization under 30%, and
- individual-card utilization lower than overall whenever possible.

For major borrowing events (mortgage or auto application), tighter utilization can help profile quality.

## Example scenario
You have:
- Card A: $5,000 limit, $1,400 balance
- Card B: $3,000 limit, $600 balance

Total utilization = $2,000 ÷ $8,000 = 25%.

If you pay $900 before statement close, utilization drops to 13.75%, which can improve reported risk profile quickly.

## How to lower utilization without closing accounts
1. Make an extra mid-cycle payment before statement closing date.
2. Move recurring spending to cards with lower utilization.
3. Request a responsible credit-limit increase (without increasing spend).
4. Avoid closing old no-fee cards if they help total available credit.

## Mistakes that backfire
- Closing cards and shrinking your total limit.
- Applying for many new cards in a short period before a loan.
- Waiting until loan week to start utilization cleanup.
