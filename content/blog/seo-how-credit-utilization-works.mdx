## Utilization is calculated two ways
Card issuers and scoring models can look at:
- **Per-card utilization** (balance/limit on each card)
- **Overall utilization** (total balances/total limits)

Both matter. A single maxed-out card can hurt even when total utilization looks moderate.

## Timing tactics that actually help
- Pay before statement close to reduce reported balance.
- Keep recurring subscriptions on one card and mid-cycle pay it down.
- Request credit-limit increases only when income supports it.

## Example
Card A limit $1,000 with $800 balance (80%).
Card B limit $9,000 with $900 balance (10%).
Overall utilization is 17%, but Card A concentration can still pressure scores.

## What not to do
- Close old no-fee cards that improve total available credit.
- Carry interest thinking it boosts score.
- Apply for multiple cards just to lower utilization quickly.
