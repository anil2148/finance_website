Credit utilization is more than one number. You can have a "good" overall ratio and still lose points if one card is heavily maxed.

## Track two ratios every month

1. **Overall utilization:** total revolving balances ÷ total limits
2. **Per-card utilization:** each card balance ÷ that card limit

Scoring systems frequently penalize high concentration on one card even when the aggregate ratio looks acceptable.

## Timing tactics that produce real movement

### Pay before statement close
Reported balances often reflect statement closing amounts. Paying 3–5 days before close can reduce reported utilization.

### Use mid-cycle payments
If spend concentrates on one card, split payments during the cycle to avoid high per-card spikes.

### Request limits strategically
A limit increase can improve utilization only when spending discipline stays stable.

## Example

- Card A: $1,200 limit, $840 balance → 70%
- Card B: $8,800 limit, $880 balance → 10%
- Overall utilization: 17%

Overall appears moderate, but Card A concentration can still hurt score trends.

## Mistakes to avoid

- Closing old no-fee cards that support total available credit.
- Carrying interest because of the myth that "it helps your score."
- Opening multiple new cards right before applying for a major loan.
