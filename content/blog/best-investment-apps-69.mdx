## Why investment apps matter in 2026

Investment apps make it easier to open and fund a Roth IRA with low minimums, recurring deposits, and beginner-friendly tools.

In 2026, strong platforms combine commission-free stock and ETF trading with fractional shares, education libraries, and automated investing options.

For new Roth IRA investors, the best app is usually one that keeps costs low and makes consistency simple.

## Best investment apps in 2026 (Roth IRA beginner focus)

| App | Best for | Key strengths | Typical costs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fidelity | First Roth IRA for long-term investors | Strong no-fee index fund lineup, educational tools, good mobile UX | $0 stock/ETF commissions; fund costs vary |
| Charles Schwab | New investors who want support options | Research tools, broad account features, branch availability | $0 stock/ETF commissions; some products may have fees |

| M1 Finance | Automated custom ETF “pie” setup | Fractional shares, recurring auto-invest, rule-based allocations | $0 trading commissions; underlying fund costs |

Always verify account fees, contribution rules, and platform features directly on the provider website before opening an account.

## How to pick the right app

1. **Prioritize long-term usability.** Pick a platform you can stay with for years, not one built around short-term hype.
2. **Compare total costs.** Review advisory fees, fund expense ratios, and optional premium subscriptions.
3. **Use automation early.** Recurring deposits and auto-invest settings help you stay consistent.
4. **Keep the portfolio simple.** Many beginners start with broad U.S. and international index exposure.

## Action plan for beginners

- Open your Roth IRA and link your checking account.
- Set an automatic monthly contribution you can sustain year-round.
- Review your allocation once per year and increase contributions after raises.
