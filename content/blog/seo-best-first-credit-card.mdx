## First-card goal: build history safely
Your first card is not about maximizing points. It is about on-time payments, low utilization, and avoiding fees you cannot offset.

## Secured vs unsecured starter cards
- **Secured card:** easier approval, requires deposit.
- **Unsecured beginner card:** no deposit, but may have stricter approval and higher APR.

If approval is uncertain, a secured card with no annual fee is often the safer starting point.

## Three filters before applying
1. Annual fee vs expected value in year one.
2. Credit limit relative to expected monthly spend.
3. Digital controls (autopay, alerts, and lock/unlock features).

## New-cardholder mistakes
- Applying to multiple cards in one week.
- Carrying a balance to "build credit" (you do not need to).
- Missing statement due dates by relying on memory.
