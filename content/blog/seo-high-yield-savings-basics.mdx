An account with a slightly higher APY is not automatically better. For emergency and short-term money, operational reliability matters as much as yield.

## APY quality checks before you compare

- Is the rate promotional or standard?
- Is there a minimum balance for the headline APY?
- Are rates tiered by balance level?
- How often has the bank changed APY over the last year?

## Access quality matters in real emergencies

Evaluate:
- External transfer timing
- Mobile app reliability and alerts
- Customer support quality and response speed
- Withdrawal or inactivity restrictions

## Example decision

- **Account A:** 4.60% APY, slower transfer reliability
- **Account B:** 4.45% APY, faster transfers and stronger support

For emergency reserves, Account B may be safer despite slightly lower APY, because delayed access during urgent expenses is expensive.

## Common mistakes

- Chasing tiny APY differences while ignoring operations.
- Keeping no checking buffer, forcing emergency transfer friction.
- Fragmenting savings across too many accounts without clear purpose.
