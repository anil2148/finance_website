A high APY headline is useful, but it is not enough for emergency cash. The right account is the one that behaves predictably when you need money in 24–48 hours.

## Use this 4-part account scorecard

Score each candidate from 1 to 5:

- **Transfer reliability:** inbound/outbound timing and limits
- **Policy transparency:** fees, holds, and statement clarity
- **Support execution:** problem resolution speed and accuracy
- **Yield durability:** promo vs long-run APY behavior

Weight transfer reliability highest if this is your emergency fund account.

## Example: two accounts, different risk profiles

Assume a $30,000 reserve.

- **Account A:** 4.60% APY, slower outbound timing, unclear hold policy
- **Account B:** 4.45% APY, consistent transfer windows, cleaner account controls

Annual interest difference is modest, but access reliability can matter more during a time-sensitive expense.

## Decision rule by cash purpose

Pick account type by job, not by marketing copy:

- **Emergency reserve:** prioritize transfer speed and policy clarity
- **Short-term goal (6–18 months):** balance APY and usability
- **Sinking fund for known bills:** value automation and sub-bucket controls

## Red flags worth rejecting immediately

- unclear ACH transfer limits in documentation
- support channels that cannot confirm transfer cut-off times
- bonus APY terms that require behavior you will not maintain
- account statements that are hard to reconcile monthly

If two red flags appear, move on.

## Operational setup after opening

Do this in the first week:

1. Link primary checking account and test a small transfer.
2. Enable low-balance and transfer alerts.
3. Create one recurring transfer aligned with payday.
4. Save screenshots/PDFs of rate and fee disclosures.

You are designing a cash system, not just opening an account.

## Final tie-breaker when accounts look similar

If two accounts score almost the same, choose the one with the easier monthly operating experience: clearer statements, faster support access, and cleaner transfer controls. Over a year, low-friction account management usually creates better savings consistency than chasing tiny APY differences.
