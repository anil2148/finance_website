## Who this is for
Anyone with cash in a traditional savings account earning low APY who wants better yield without taking market risk.

## Why this matters right now
In 2026, the gap between low-yield savings and competitive online accounts is still large:
- Typical national savings averages remain low relative to top offers.
- Competitive HYSAs are still often in the **4.5%–5.0% APY** range.

On a $10,000 balance, the spread can mean hundreds of dollars in annual interest difference.

## How to choose a HYSA
Prioritize the following:
1. **FDIC/NCUA insurance** (up to applicable limits)
2. **No monthly maintenance fee**
3. **No restrictive minimum balance you cannot maintain**
4. **Competitive variable APY**
5. **Fast transfer times** (commonly 1–2 business days)

## Key tradeoffs
- Higher yields are often online-only (limited branch access).
- Rates are variable and can drop if benchmark rates fall.
- Convenience features differ by provider (mobile tools, transfer limits, support).

## Example scenario
A saver with $12,000 at 0.40% APY earns about $48/year (before tax). At 4.80% APY, that is about $576/year. The yield spread is meaningful for emergency funds and short-term goals.

## Common mistakes
- Chasing teaser APY without reading balance rules
- Keeping emergency savings below competitive yields for long periods
- Opening too many accounts and losing visibility over cash allocation

## Next steps
1. Check your current APY inside your bank dashboard.
2. Compare at least three HYSA offers.
3. Move an initial tranche and test transfer speed.
4. Automate monthly contributions.
