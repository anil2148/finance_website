A slightly higher APY does not automatically make one account better. In practice, transfer speed, withdrawal friction, and platform reliability often matter more for emergency and short-term savings.

## APY quality checklist
Before comparing rates, verify:

- Is APY promotional or ongoing?
- Is there a minimum balance to earn the advertised rate?
- Are there tiered rates that lower yield for part of your balance?

## Access and operations checklist
- External transfer timeline (same day vs 2–5 days)
- Mobile app stability and alerts
- Customer support channels/hours
- Account restrictions (withdrawal limits, dormancy policies)

## Example decision
- **Account A:** 4.60% APY, slow transfer timing
- **Account B:** 4.45% APY, fast transfers and better support

For emergency reserves, Account B may be the stronger choice because access reliability can outweigh a small APY gap.

## Common errors
- Chasing tiny APY differences while ignoring operational friction
- Holding zero checking buffer, forcing emergency transfers
- Spreading savings across too many banks without a clear purpose
