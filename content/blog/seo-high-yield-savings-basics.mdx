A high APY is useful, but access speed and account reliability matter more when this cash is your safety buffer.

## The 5-question HYSA filter

Before opening an account, answer these with evidence:

1. **How quickly can money reach checking when needed?**
2. **Does APY hold up or reset after promo windows?**
3. **Are there balance tiers or activity rules that reduce yield?**
4. **Can support resolve account issues quickly?**
5. **Do alerts and buckets match your cash-management workflow?**

## Practical scoring template (100 points)

- Access speed: 30
- Yield durability: 25
- Rules/limits clarity: 20
- Support reliability: 15
- Tools and controls: 10

Reject anything below 70 for core emergency cash.

## Decision by cash purpose

- **Emergency reserve:** access speed + reliability
- **12-month planned goal:** yield + account tools
- **Bill buffer:** keep in checking for immediate use
