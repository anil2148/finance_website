Most people do not lose money on one big mistake—they lose **$9.99 here and $19.99 there** on autopay they stopped valuing months ago.

A subscription audit system helps you shut down hidden monthly leakage fast, without tracking every coffee or overhauling your whole budget.

## A fast scenario: what 30 minutes can actually save

Jordan ran a quick subscription audit on a Sunday afternoon and found an unused workout app, two overlapping streaming plans, and an old cloud-storage add-on.

- Total cut: **$96/month**
- Annual impact: **$1,152/year**
- Time spent: **about 30 minutes**

A realistic result for most households is saving **$80 to $150 per month** from one focused audit.

## The 30-minute subscription audit checklist

Work through this checklist in order:

1. **Bank statements (last 90 days):** scan repeating merchants and same-dollar charges.
2. **Credit-card statements:** check every card, including backup cards you rarely use.
3. **App stores:** open Apple/Google subscriptions and review active renewals.
4. **PayPal and wallet autopay:** inspect pre-approved payments and recurring billing agreements.
5. **In-app subscriptions:** check memberships managed inside services (fitness, media, AI, productivity).

For each item, label it **Keep**, **Downgrade**, or **Cancel now**.

## Keep, downgrade, or cancel rules

Use a simple decision filter:

- **Keep** if you used it in the last 30 days and would buy it again today.
- **Downgrade** if you use it occasionally but premium features are unnecessary.
- **Cancel** if you have not used it in 45+ days or it duplicates another service.

This keeps the process practical and avoids vague “I might use it later” decisions.

## Put the savings to work immediately

Do not leave reclaimed cash in checking where it disappears into daily spending.

- Move the exact monthly savings amount to a named goal.
- Use one transfer date after payday.
- Track progress in a spending or budget tool.

Use the [Budget Planner](/calculators/budget-planner) to see where this reclaimed cash should go next.

## Add a once-per-quarter nudge

Most people only audit once, then drift back into subscription sprawl. Set a recurring calendar event:

**"Subscription Audit (30 min) — every quarter"**

Quarterly rhythm is frequent enough to catch drift and light enough to sustain.

## What to do next

1. Run the checklist today.
2. Cancel and downgrade immediately.
3. Schedule your quarterly nudge now.
4. Expand this process to all spending categories.
