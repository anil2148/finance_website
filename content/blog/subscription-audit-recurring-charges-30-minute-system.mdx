If you’ve ever seen a surprise charge from an old app, streaming service, or “free trial” you forgot to cancel, this is your reset.

Use this page to find every recurring charge, make a clear keep/cancel/downgrade decision, and cut low-value subscriptions in one sitting.

## 30-minute audit process

1. **Open** your last 30–90 days of bank and credit-card transactions.
2. **Scan** every transaction line and highlight each recurring charge.
3. **Check** Apple App Store, Google Play, and PayPal automatic payments.
4. **List** each subscription with price, billing cycle, and last time you used it.
5. **Tag** each item as keep, downgrade, or cancel before opening account settings.
6. **Cancel** unused services immediately and **downgrade** borderline plans in the same session.
7. **Set** a calendar reminder for your next audit in 90 days.

## What you’ll find

Most people find at least one of these quickly:

- **2–3 unused subscriptions at $10–$20/month each** → roughly **$300–$700/year**.
- **Duplicate entertainment services** (for example, Netflix + Hulu + Prime with overlapping use) that can cut **$12–$40/month**.
- **Forgotten free trials** that rolled into paid plans, often **$7–$25/month** per service.
- **Legacy app add-ons** billed monthly even after the app stopped being used.

## Scenarios: make the decision fast

### 1) Unused fitness app
You’re paying **$14.99/month** and used it once in the last 60 days.

👉 In this situation, **canceling the $14.99/month subscription is usually the better move because it saves about $180/year with almost no active use**.

### 2) Two overlapping streaming services
You pay **$15.49/month** for one service and **$11.99/month** for another, but you mostly watch one platform.

👉 In this situation, **canceling the lower-use service is usually the better move because cutting one plan saves about $144–$186/year without reducing your primary viewing**.

### 3) Cloud plan priced above your usage
You pay **$9.99/month** for 2TB storage but use less than 200GB and could move to a **$2.99/month** tier.

👉 In this situation, **downgrading is usually the better move because it keeps the service you use and cuts about $84/year**.

## Cancel vs keep vs downgrade

- **Cancel** → if you use it less than once per month.
- **Downgrade** → if you use it occasionally but not enough to justify full price.
- **Keep** → if you use it weekly or it replaces a higher-cost alternative.

## 15–30 minute action plan

1. Open bank and credit-card transactions.
2. List every recurring charge.
3. Cancel unused subscriptions.
4. Downgrade borderline services.
5. Keep only subscriptions used weekly or replacing a higher-cost option.
6. Set a reminder for the next audit.

## Keep the system running

Run one full audit now, then repeat every quarter. A recurring reminder keeps new charges from piling up.
